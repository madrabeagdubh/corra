We walk the ruin,
cheerful in the morning light saying
Look, there is nothing there.
There never was.

It is a small matter, being Irish.
No more than praise the stone for falling downward.
But to be Gaelic, that is another labour entirely.
It is a thing chosen. A road taken when other roads lay open.

And for every Gael that breathes this island’s air, ten wait in other lands.
Ten who have heard one phrase of the old tongue and felt quiet in a way they could not explain.

There are those who say this way is finished,
these customs are the smoke of what was burned.
Let them say it.

It is only a surly person that would not enquire.
To the one who feels the pull of it,
Joy to you, and from you, to all who come after.
